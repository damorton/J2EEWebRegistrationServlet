Learning journeys and learner voices - promoting innovative pedagogies through new technologies.

Gráinne Conole
University of Leicester

New technologies offer a rich variety of ways in which learners can interact with learning materials and communicate and collaborate with others. Mobile devices means learning anywhere anytime is now a reality. The talk will provide an overview of different technologies and how they can facilitate different pedagogical approaches. However, learners and teachers lack the necessary digital literacies to harness the power of new media. A new approach to Learning Design will be described, which aims to help practitioners make more informed design decisions that are pedagogically effective and make innovative use of new technologies.

Biography

Gráinne Conole is Professor of learning innovation at Leicester University. Her research interests include the use, integration and evaluation of technologies and their impact on organisational change. Two of her interests are learning design and Open Educational Resources. She serves on and chairs a number of national and international advisory boards, steering groups, committees and international conference programmes. She has published and presented nearly 1000 conference proceedings, workshops and articles and keeps a blog www.e4innovation.com. She has a PhD in X-Ray Crystallography. She is author of a new Springer entitled: ‘Designing for learning in an open world’. 



“Creating Spaces for Student Voices”

Catherine Cronin
NUI Galway

“To exist, humanly, is to name the world, to change it. Once named, the world in its turn reappears to the namers as a problem and requires of them a new naming. Human beings are not built in silence, but in word, in work, in action-reflection.”— Paulo Freire

Human beings are not built in silence. We delight in the first sounds of babies, the first words of children; we marvel at their acquisition of language. And in classrooms, at all levels of education, what do we ask of growing children and adults? Too often, while we speak, we ask for silence. The architecture of most of our classrooms and lecture halls both reflects and contributes to this. As educators, many of us have rediscovered the power of word, work and action-reflection in our own learning. We speak, we write, we use social media to share and to engage in dialogue. A growing number of educators are inviting students to do the same. Creating opportunities for students to find and share their voices requires openness and a willingness to challenge long-held assumptions and practices – those of our students and institutions as well as ourselves. Catherine will share the voices of students, from all levels of education, as well as her work and the work of other open educators, as she explores ways to create spaces for student voices.

Biography:  

Catherine Cronin is Academic Coordinator of online IT programmes and Lecturer [sic] in Information Technology at the National University of Ireland, Galway. Catherine’s work focuses on open education, digital literacies and social media in education. Catherine is a contributor at many education conferences and was an invited keynote speaker at the 2012 Galway Symposium on Higher Education where she spoke about the challenges of open education for higher education. In addition to her work in HE, Catherine works with students, teachers and parents at both primary and second level to explore e-safety and social media for learning. She is a long-time advocate for girls and women in technology, working to challenge gender stereotypes and change gendered cultures. Happiest when crossing boundaries and bridging cultures, Catherine is a New Yorker living in Galway; has degrees in Mechanical Engineering, Systems Engineering and Women’s Studies; has taught in community settings, the Open University and HE in Ireland and Scotland; and is a passionate advocate for the power of social media to connect us and to transform learning.

“Empowering Student Voices through Innovation”

Martha Rotter

Classrooms today are being flipped upside down by the addition of digital content and new portable devices. How can educators take advantage of these new tools and use them to enhance learning instead of distract? How can students understand how to make their voices heard, both in the classroom and further afield? Martha Rotter walks you through some of the most game-changing ideas in this space today.

Biography:  

Martha Rotter is a developer and technologist based in Dublin, Ireland, focused on the digital publishing space. Through her tech startup company, Woopie (http://woop.ie), she helps educators, students, publishers, corporations and independent writers to create content and learning materials that are beautiful and work great on any device and platform. After co-founding Idea magazine in 2011, she wanted to help other people make beautiful reading experiences too, and began working on Woopie full-time.

Martha has been a part-time computer science lecturer at National College Ireland and the Digital Skills Academy for a number of years and was formerly the CTO of a New York based digital publisher. From 2001-2010 she was a software engineer, technical manager and developer evangelist at Microsoft. She holds bachelor's degrees in computer science, business administration, and international studies, and has done graduate work in the areas of natural language processing, data visualization and human computer interaction. In her spare time she works with community tech groups like Age Action, OpenCoffee Dublin, and women's technology initiatives. She is on twitter at @martharotter and blogs about technology and publishing at martharotter.com/blog.
